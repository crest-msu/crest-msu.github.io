Thrust 1: Geospatial Technologies

  • will harness big data, including from satellite remote sensing, ground-based sensors, and IoT networks to streamline real-time observations of the AWT into the DT, and capture the historical (past four decades) and current state of the atmosphere (temperature & precipitation), hydrosphere (surface & subsurface water systems), cryosphere (glaciers), and geosphere (land use & land cover and soils), feeding directly into the modeling framework to drive, constrain, and validate its components (elaborated below). Lead: Qi; Collaborators: Mantha, Pokhrel, Tan, and Research Accociate-1 (RA-1; TBD).

Thrust 2: Integrated WEF Nexus Modeling

  • will model the hydrological, agricultural, and ecological systems of the AWT at the hyper-resolution scale (100 m-1 km), capturing the key interactions among the previously mentioned spheres. The modeling framework will account for the complex dynamics of climatic (e.g., temperature rise, glacier melt, and changes in monsoon dynamics) and human (e.g., population growth, land use change and deforestation, agricultural intensification, hydropower development, migration and resettlement, and water governance) drivers, capturing the transboundary complexities in WEF dynamics. The framework will form the core of the AWT-DT. Lead: Pokhrel; Collaborators: Aho, Mantha, Pueppke, Qi, Tarabara, and RA-1.

Thrust 3: Socioeconomic Dynamics

  • will focus on the anthroposphere, examining the interplay between climate, human, and environmental systems in the AWT, with an emphasis on governance, policy frameworks, and social structures. It will integrate AI-driven socioeconomic analytics with econometric models to explore how climate change, migration, land use transitions, agricultural practices, and resource management, both shape and are shaped by societal behaviors, institutional frameworks, and decision-making processes. These models and outcomes will constitute a major part of the DT metaverse to enable stakeholder engagement to adopt and apply new knowledge and tools in addressing climate resilience and WEF challenges. Lead: Ortega; Collaborators: Bennett, Flaim, Gulati, Ahmad, and RA-2 (TBD).

Thrust 4: Integrated Data Analytics

  • will leverage the latest advances in computational science and technologies to integrate AI algorithms, IoTs, models, and Earth observation data to capture real-time evolution of geospatial patterns such as glacial melt rates, land use transitions, and socioeconomic dynamics, optimize process-based models, and enhance the predictive capabilities of the models, hence the AWT-DT to generate realistic scenarios for actions. It will generate new knowledge on patterns and processes occurring among the interconnected systems, which is an important function of the AWT-DT. Lead: Tan; Collaborators: Mantha, Pokhrel, Qi, and RA-1.

Thrust 5: DT Metaverse and Disaster Risk Management and Resilience

  • will construct the DT metaverse framework to enable use-inspired analyses and adoptions with a focus on advanced strategies and tools for disaster risk management, including large-scale water balance assessment and prediction, flood mapping and risk analysis, agricultural water management, critical fisheries and aquaculture management, evaluation of livelihood climate impacts, socioeconomic dynamics and future WEF scenarios. These capabilities will enable stakeholders (e.g., policymakers, emergency responders, and resource managers) to assess and predict disaster risks while designing tailored strategies to mitigate climate impacts. The strategy is to shift disaster management from a reactive to a proactive approach, protecting infrastructure, livelihoods, and vulnerable communities across the AWT region and beyond. Co-Leads: Pokhrel & Qi; Collaborators: Ortega, Pueppke, Tarabara, Wargo, RA-2, and Asia Hub partners.
