About

I'm a hydrologist specializing in hydrological and climate modeling, studying climate change impacts and human influence on global water resources.

My current research is focused on the study of the influence of groundwater, irrigation, and climate change on global water resources using hydrological models and investigating the hydrologic changes in the Mekong River Basin (under the Belmont Forum Project, funded by the NSF).

I completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Gandhinagar, India. Throughout my doctoral studies, I focused on developing a hydrologic outlook for India, with a specific emphasis on monitoring and forecasting reservoir storage, floods, and droughts. Prior to my current position, I had the opportunity to contribute to a UNICEF project that aimed to develop a comprehensive hydroclimatic framework and forecasting system tailored for India.
